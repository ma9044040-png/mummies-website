I. The Mortuary Sciences

Ancient Egyptians were the world's first biologists. They didn't wrap bodies for fun; they were trying to turn a human into a "Vessel for Eternity." If the body rotted, the soul became a "lost ghost."

[attachment_0](attachment)

The Bio-Chemistry of Natron

They used Natron (a mix of salt and baking soda). It wasn't just a surface dry-out; it worked at a cellular level to stop the enzymes that cause rotting. They stuffed the body with spice-soaked linen to keep the shape, because without it, the body would collapse like a deflated balloon.

The Organ Extraction

The priests used a bronze hook to whisk the brain and pull it out the nose (gross, but effective!). They kept the Heart inside the body, believing it was the center of intelligence. The rest went into Canopic Jars guarded by the sons of Horus.

III. The Divine Pantheon

The Gods were serious business. If you didn't keep them happy, the Nile wouldn't flood, and everyone would starve. Each god had a "Job Description" and a specific animal avatar.

Ra: The Solar Commander

Ra sails his Solar Barque across the sky. He represents the sun at its peak. Every night, he travels through the 12 gates of the underworld, fighting Apep, the snake of chaos. If Ra wins, we get a sunrise. If he loses? Total darkness forever.

Anubis: Master of Secrets

The Jackal god. He is the ultimate judge of the dead. He invented mummification when he helped Isis put Osiris back together. He is the only god allowed to touch the "Scale of Truth."

Sobek: Lord of the Waters

The Crocodile god. He represents the wild power of the Nile. Egyptians were so scared of crocodiles that they worshipped them to stay safe. They even had a city called Crocodilopolis where a tame crocodile lived in a pool with gold earrings!
